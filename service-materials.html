Advanced Energy Materials

My materials science expertise focuses on high-temperature superconductors and their behavior under extreme conditions, particularly relevant for fusion energy and advanced power applications.

From fundamental characterization to radiation damage recovery, I understand materials at the atomic level and their macroscopic properties.

High-Temperature Superconductor Research

Specialized expertise in REBCO (Rare Earth Barium Copper Oxide) superconductors, the leading materials for high-field magnets and energy applications.

  • REBCO Superconductors: In-depth research on Gadolinium Barium Copper Oxide (GdBCO) and related compounds for fusion magnet applications.
  • Critical Temperature Measurement: Precision determination of superconducting transition temperatures using 17T cryogenic systems.
  • Transport Properties: Comprehensive electrical characterization including resistive transitions and current carrying capacity assessments.
  • Structure-Property Relationships: Understanding how crystal structure, defects, and processing affect superconducting performance.
Research Work at TRIGA Reactor

Neutron Irradiation Effects & Recovery

Understanding radiation damage mechanisms and recovery processes critical for fusion reactor applications and space technologies.

  • Radiation Damage Mechanisms: Study of how neutron irradiation creates defects in superconductor crystal lattices and affects performance.
  • Thermal Annealing Recovery: Investigation of heat treatment processes to restore superconducting properties after irradiation damage.
  • In-Situ Monitoring: Real-time measurement of property recovery during thermal treatments using custom instrumentation.
  • Fusion Relevance: Research directly applicable to superconducting magnets in fusion reactors where neutron fluence is significant.

Advanced Materials Characterization

Comprehensive suite of characterization techniques for understanding materials properties and performance optimization.

  • Electrical Characterization: Four-point probe measurements, Van der Pauw techniques, and resistance vs. temperature analysis.
  • Cryogenic Testing: Low-temperature measurements from 4K to 300K using superconducting magnet systems up to 17T.
  • Magnetic Field Dependencies: Investigation of critical parameters under varying magnetic field conditions.
  • Statistical Analysis: Comprehensive data analysis with uncertainty quantification and reproducibility assessments.

Materials Processing & Preparation

Practical experience with materials processing techniques essential for research and device fabrication.

  • Chemical Etching: Development of etching procedures for superconductor surface preparation and complex pattern fabrication into superconductors.
  • Protective Atmosphere Processing: Encapsulation techniques under protective atmospheres and flame sealing in quartz tubes.
  • Contact Manufacturing: Fabrication of electrical contacts for precise resistance measurements and device applications.
  • Annealing Optimization: Development of thermal treatment protocols for property recovery and enhancement.
